## What is PAI?

PAI stands for **Personal AI Infrastructure**. Daniel Miessler built it. It's open-source. It lives here: [github.com/danielmiessler/PAI](https://github.com/danielmiessler/fabric).

The core idea: your AI shouldn't be a stranger. It should know your goals, your values, your context, your history, your voice. Every session shouldn't start cold — it should start *informed.*

Most people treat AI like a vending machine. You insert a question, you get an answer. Transaction complete. The machine forgets you the moment you walk away.

PAI treats AI like a **partner**. One that reads your files, knows your story, and gets smarter about you over time.

## What is TELOS?

TELOS is my Life Operating System document.

It's currently about 20,000 tokens of concentrated Gray — my missions, my values, my non-negotiables, my goals, my NIDs (New Identity Declarations), my career context, my family situation, my strengths, my growth areas.

It is, genuinely, the most important document I own.

Here's the dad joke version: My computer has a BIOS that boots before anything else runs. My life needed a BIOS too. So I wrote one. I called it TELOS — from the Greek word for *purpose* and *ultimate end.*

My AI reads it. Not summarized. Not chunked. The whole thing. Before we talk about anything.

## What Did I Actually Build?

I forked Daniel's PAI structure and adapted it into my own setup:

- **TELOS** — my core identity document (20k+ tokens, never edited by AI, only by me)
- **Memory files** — auto-saved behavioral notes from sessions
- **HANDOFF.md** — the session bridge document (more on that in Tentacle #2)
- **Bodhi** — my Claude AI instance with custom session protocols
- **Open Brain** — a self-hosted vector memory VPS (Tentacle #4)

The whole thing is a distributed intelligence system where *I* am the architect and the AI is the engine.
