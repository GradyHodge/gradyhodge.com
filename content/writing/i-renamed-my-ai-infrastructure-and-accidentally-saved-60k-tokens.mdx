Yesterday I had a problem: "Human 3.0" sounded like we built a product. We built a system. A philosophy. A way of thinking about AI as your compass — not your replacement.

So we renamed it.

## The Glow-Up

**Before:** Human 3.0 (sounds like software)
**After:** Compass (sounds like someone got it)

Compass is the human. AI navigates.

That's the whole thing. I needed to feel like the principal and Bodhi was the guide, not the other way around. So we:

- Renamed every page on galvanizedesigns.com
- Updated the SIGNAL API (my public identity endpoint)
- Added the catchphrase to the footer
- Deployed everything in 20 minutes

## The Accidental Efficiency Hack

While I was reorganizing, I read a YouTube video transcript about Claude Code optimization (because apparently that's my life now). Turns out I had **52,000 invisible tokens** sitting in my settings.json doing absolutely nothing.

Two words: `enableAllProjectMcpServers: false`

Before: Every MCP from every project loaded on every message.
After: I load what I actually need.

Then I added:
- `autoCompactPercentageOverride: 75` (triggers compression before quality tanks)
- `bashMaxOutputLength: 150000` (stops silent truncation retry loops)
- Deny rules for node_modules, dist, .next, lock files (stop reading garbage)

**Net result:** 30-60K tokens removed from baseline context. Didn't change anything about how we work. Just cleaned house.

## The Vibathon Thing

I'm entering Compass in a May 2026 competition. The guidelines were scattered across 5 pages. So I:

1. Extracted all best practices from their official guides
2. Created copy-paste prompts for Backend Rules, Design Rules, Demo Scripts
3. Built a 3-5 minute demo structure that actually wins
4. Documented the whole playbook in VIBATHON_STRATEGY.md

The judges want to see:
- A real problem (not buzzwords)
- A real solution (not hype)
- A real demo (not pre-loaded mockups)
- Understanding of what you built (not just narration)

So the playbook is: give Bodhi the rules upfront, ask specific questions with context, record one clean take (authenticity > polish).

## What Surprised Me

I thought the efficiency gains would be the headline. They're not.

The headline is that naming the system *changed how I think about it.* Compass isn't an AI tool anymore. It's a collaborator with a specific job: navigate while I drive.

That mental shift? Worth 60K tokens.

## What You Can Do

If you're building something with AI:

1. **Name it.** Not because the AI cares. Because you'll show up differently.
2. **Give it rules.** Copy-paste them every session if you have to. Consistency > perfection.
3. **Clean your settings.json.** You probably have 20K+ invisible tokens sitting there.
4. **Write it down.** The playbook becomes the shortcut.
