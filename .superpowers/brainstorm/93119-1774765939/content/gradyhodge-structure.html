gradyhodge.com — Site Structure

Three options for how the site is organized. Each serves the Fractional CAIO audience differently.

A

The Personal OS

One long homepage that covers everything — like a personal website that replaces LinkedIn, shows your work, and lets your personality show. Simple, scroll-driven.

Sections (top to bottom):
Hero: name + one-liner + "Book a Call" CTA
About: origin story, recovery, AI awakening
Work: featured projects (Recon AI, GalvanizeDesigns)
Writing: latest articles (PAI, Fabric, experiments)
Experiments: Network Chuck labs, AI builds
Hobbies: board games, quirky IRL stuff
Connect: links to GitHub, LinkedIn, Instagram, contact
Best for: LinkedIn replacement · personal brand · SEO
B

The Authority Hub

Structured like a thought leader's site — homepage is a command center, articles are first-class citizens, everything points toward the retainer conversation.

Pages:
/ → Hero + recent writing + featured work + CTA
/about → Full story, philosophy, Telos
/writing → Article index (PAI, AI GEO, experiments)
/work → Case studies (Recon AI, etc.)
/lab → Network Chuck experiments + builds
/human → Hobbies, IRL, board games
/contact → Book a call + retainer inquiry
Best for: Fractional CAIO positioning · article SEO · AI GEO
C

The Hybrid

Single homepage like Option A, but with separate article and lab sections like Option B. Best of both — personality on the homepage, depth for those who go deeper.

Homepage + 3 deep sections:
/ → Scroll-driven: hero · about · work · connect
/writing → Article index + individual articles
/lab → Experiments (Network Chuck, PAI, Fabric)
/human → Hobbies + IRL personality

Everything cross-links back to galvanizedesigns.com
Best for: balance of personality + authority + SEO depth

Click your preferred structure. You can mix elements — nothing is fixed yet.
